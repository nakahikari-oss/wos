Canyon Clash Basics

Video by: Karin Category: Events

This video, created by Karin, covers the basic concepts and techniques of Canyon Clash.

English CC subtitles are available — click the CC button in the YouTube player.

Video Transcript

Hello everyone, I'm Karin.
In this video, I'll briefly go over some key tips for Canyon Clash.

Squad Bar: Advance & Retreat

Once you enter the battlefield, besides keeping an eye on the battle channel,
please pay attention to the squad bar below your avatar in the upper left of the screen.
When the number of troops waiting inside a building exceeds 5, you can Advance or Retreat.
Remember — do not crowd inside buildings and wait idly.

Reinforce Your Troops

In the same squad bar, you'll also see Reinforce and Speedup icons.
Tapping on a building likewise shows the Advance and Reinforce options.
Whenever the Reinforce icon appears, always make sure to top up your troops.
Fighting with depleted troops puts you at a serious disadvantage — reviving them wastes valuable time.

Checking Scores

To check your personal and alliance scores, tap the area to the right of your avatar.

Guardian Role & Fuel

The first icon in the bottom right of the screen lets you view Guardian assignments.
Fuel is refilled from here as well. When appointed as a Guardian,
refueling speed varies depending on the building level.
The fuel tank cap is 300,000. If you notice it's full,
immediately notify the on-site Commander or R4 to adjust: "Move the player with full fuel off the top slot — prioritize those who need fuel."

Battle Phase Alerts

The system will notify you of each battle phase.
In addition to the text and countdown timer at the very top of the screen,
there's also a notification icon on the right: "Icebound Palace is about to open."
Even if no one reminds you, always stay alert for attack commands.

Using the Minimap

Tap the small icon in the bottom left of the minimap to expand it.
Press and drag on the minimap to quickly survey the battlefield.
Before the Icebound Palace opens, make sure your advance route is clear.

Icebound Palace — The Most Contested Objective

While capturing enemy positions also earns points, the Icebound Palace gives the most.
It can even flip the result at the final tally — it is truly a must-contest objective.

Speedup Usage

If Healing Speedups are what you spend in Foundry Battle,
March Speedups are what you'll burn through fastest in Canyon Clash.

Reading Buildings from the Inside

Another battlefield tip: tapping into a building not only shows Reinforce —
you can also read the battle status to understand the attack/defense situation and whether squads need to be topped up.
You can also check the progress bar at the bottom of the building.
When the number of allied squads decreases, your supply route may have been cut.
Check back — sure enough, the supply route had been severed.

Pre-Assigned Routes & the Commander Role

Finally, if your alliance has assigned groups in advance,
make sure to remember your assigned route and always monitor the battle channel.
Follow the commander's direction.
The Commander's main role is refueling — don't be misled by the title.
When appointing a Commander, do not select high-combat players — battle command is a separate role.

Canyon Clash is one of my personal favorite events, second only to Foundry Battle.
The rewards are great too. I hope everyone finds enjoyment in this event!
